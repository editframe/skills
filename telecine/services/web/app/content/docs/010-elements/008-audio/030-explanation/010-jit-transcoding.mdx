## JIT Transcoding: Instant Audio Playback Without Preprocessing

Editframe's Just-In-Time (JIT) transcoding enables playback of audio URLs without downloading or preprocessing the entire file. This architecture addresses engineering challenges inherent to native audio playback while reducing latency and bandwidth costs.

### Engineering Challenges with Native Audio Playback

Using native audio playback with original files presents these engineering challenges: Native playback requires full file download before seeking, making previews slow. Supporting multiple quality levels typically requires preprocessing infrastructure to transcode and store variants upfront. Seeking is often slow and unresponsive, which can discourage exploration in audio editing workflows.

### The Solution: On-Demand Audio Generation

Instead of preprocessing entire audio files, Editframe generates audio data on-demand as users need it. Playback begins typically within seconds—users hear audio quickly without waiting for full file downloads. Only the portions users actually listen to are fetched, significantly reducing bandwidth costs. Seeking to any position is typically very fast with immediate audio feedback, making scrubbing responsive and encouraging exploration.

**Architectural advantages:**
- **Fast startup:** Playback begins typically within seconds without downloading entire files
- **Efficient bandwidth:** Only listened portions are fetched, reducing costs by orders of magnitude
- **Fast seeking:** Very fast seeking with immediate audio feedback
- **No preprocessing:** Works with most standard audio URLs typically within seconds, minimal infrastructure setup required
- **Automatic optimization:** Quality levels are selected automatically for optimal playback

### Benefits for Your Workflow

**Development speed:** Test audio changes typically within seconds without preprocessing delays. Iterate quickly and preview audio typically within seconds after upload.

**Cost efficiency:** Pay only for audio data users actually listen to. No storage costs for unused quality levels, reduced bandwidth costs through efficient delivery.

**User experience:** Audio starts playing typically within seconds, rather than after long loading times. Smooth, responsive seeking encourages exploration and can lead to higher engagement.

**Simplified architecture:** Minimal preprocessing infrastructure to build or maintain. Works with standard audio URLs without special setup. Focus on your application logic, not audio infrastructure.
