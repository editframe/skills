## Tutorial: Your First Video Composition

This tutorial will guide you through the essential concepts of working with video elements in Editframe. Each step builds on the previous one, teaching you the fundamental skills needed for video composition.

### Step 1: Display a Basic Video

Let's start with the simplest possible video composition - displaying a single video.

**What you'll learn:** How to place a video element in a composition container.

<Demonstration layout="horizontal" defaultSourceVisible alwaysShowSource>
  <Timegroup mode="contain" className="w-[720px] h-[480px] flex items-center justify-center bg-black overflow-hidden">
    <Video src="https://assets.editframe.com/bars-n-tone.mp4" className="size-full object-contain" />
  </Timegroup>
</Demonstration>

**Try it yourself:** Change the `className` to use `object-cover` instead of `object-contain` to see how the video fills the container differently.

### Step 2: Trim Your Video

Now let's learn how to show only part of a video. Editframe supports two approaches to trimming.

**What you'll learn:** How to use both relative trimming (`trimstart`/`trimend`) and absolute trimming (`sourcein`/`sourceout`).

**Relative trimming** matches common video editors where you drag handles inward from the edges. The source media is 10 seconds long. By setting `trimstart="2s"` and `trimend="2s"`, we remove 2 seconds from each end, resulting in a 6-second clip.

<div className="flex justify-center">
  <img src="/assets/trim-semantics.png" className="w-1/2" alt="10 second source media, trimmed to 6 seconds with relative trimstart and trimend" />
</div>

<Demonstration layout="horizontal" defaultSourceVisible alwaysShowSource>
  <Timegroup mode="contain" className="w-[720px] h-[480px] flex items-center justify-center bg-black overflow-hidden">
    <Video src="https://assets.editframe.com/bars-n-tone.mp4" trimstart="2s" trimend="2s" className="size-full object-contain" />
  </Timegroup>
</Demonstration>

**Absolute trimming** gives you precise control when you know exact timestamps. By setting `sourcein="2s"` and `sourceout="4s"`, we show exactly the 2-second window from 2s to 4s in the source.

<div className="flex justify-center">
  <img src="/assets/src-semantics.png" className="w-1/2" alt="10 second source media, trimmed to 2 seconds with absolute sourcein and sourceout markers" />
</div>

<Demonstration layout="horizontal" defaultSourceVisible alwaysShowSource>
  <Timegroup mode="contain" className="w-[720px] h-[480px] flex items-center justify-center bg-black overflow-hidden">
    <Video src="https://assets.editframe.com/bars-n-tone.mp4" sourcein="2s" sourceout="4s" className="size-full object-contain" />
  </Timegroup>
</Demonstration>

**Try it yourself:** Experiment with both approaches. For help choosing which to use, see [How to Choose Between trimStart and sourceIn](/docs/elements/video/how-to/choose-trimming). For deeper understanding, see the [Trimming Semantics explanation](/docs/elements/video/explanation/trimming-semantics).

### Step 3: Create a Simple Sequence

Now let's combine multiple video clips to play one after another.

**What you'll learn:** How to create sequential video playback using `mode="sequence"` on a timegroup.

<Demonstration layout="horizontal" defaultSourceVisible alwaysShowSource>
  <Timegroup mode="sequence" className="w-[720px] h-[480px] flex items-center justify-center bg-black overflow-hidden">
    <Video src="https://assets.editframe.com/bars-n-tone.mp4" sourcein="0s" sourceout="2s" className="size-full object-contain" />
    <Video src="https://assets.editframe.com/bars-n-tone.mp4" sourcein="8s" className="size-full object-contain" />
    <Video src="https://assets.editframe.com/bars-n-tone.mp4" sourcein="2s" sourceout="4s" className="size-full object-contain" />
  </Timegroup>
</Demonstration>

**Try it yourself:** Add a fourth video to the sequence. Notice how the timeline automatically extends to accommodate it.

### Step 4: Layer Videos with Content

The most powerful compositions combine video with text, graphics, and other elements using layers.

**What you'll learn:** How to use nested timegroups, absolute positioning, and z-index to layer content over video backgrounds.

**Practical application:** This technique is essential for creating titles, captions, overlays, and branded content.

In this example, each nested timegroup contains both a video (positioned absolutely as background) and text (positioned relatively with a higher z-index).

<Demonstration layout="horizontal" defaultSourceVisible alwaysShowSource>
  <Timegroup mode="sequence" className="w-[720px] h-[480px] flex items-center justify-center bg-black overflow-hidden">
    <Timegroup class="flex flex-col items-center justify-center">
      <Video src="https://assets.editframe.com/bars-n-tone.mp4" sourcein="0s" sourceout="2s" className="z-0 absolute top-0 left-0 size-full object-contain" />
      <h1 className="relative bg-blue-500 text-4xl p-2 text-white">First in sequence</h1>
    </Timegroup>
    <Timegroup class="flex flex-col items-center justify-center">
      <Video src="https://assets.editframe.com/bars-n-tone.mp4" sourcein="8s" className="z-0 absolute top-0 left-0 size-full object-contain" />
      <h1 className="relative bg-blue-500 text-4xl p-2 text-white">Second in sequence</h1>
    </Timegroup>
  </Timegroup>
</Demonstration>

**Key technique:** The video uses `absolute` positioning with `z-0` to act as a background, while the text uses `relative` positioning with a higher z-index to appear on top.
